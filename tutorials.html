Paper presentation "Loss of H3K9 Methylation"

The abstract highlights the significance of Histone H3 lysine 9 (H3K9) methylation in defining heterochromatin across organisms. Using CRISPR/Cas9, the study disrupted six SET domain enzymes responsible for H3K9 methylation in mouse embryonic fibroblasts (MEFs), leading to loss of methylation, derepression of repeat elements, genomic instability, and disruption of heterochromatin organization. This underscores the essential role of H3K9 methylation in maintaining genome integrity and heterochromatin structure.
